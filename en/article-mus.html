“Calling”, a deep message

Posted the : 18/10/2023

Reading time : 3 min

The song “Calling” from the film’s original soundtrack is a song that covers importanttopics from the film such as sacrifice and dedication.

The song is sung by 4 American rappers: Metro Boomin, A boogie wit da hoodie, Swae Lee and Nav. These artists made a lot of feats for the film’s soundtrack. Actually, Metro Boomin was the main artist in charge of the film’s audio even with other rappers. Calling is a very striking music in the film because it’s message is sincere and expresses well the feelings of Miles towards the one that he loves: Gwen Stacey.

Lyrics showing a great determination

The lyrics of the video reveals a strong desire to protect and take care of the loved one, even at the price of his own needs and integrity. Here, Miles Morales presents himself like a hero ready to save her although he already have the important task of being Spiderman and protecting his city. That being said, he insists on the fact that he is ready to overcome obstacles and challenges for her and expresses his availability to support her. The song tackles a darker mood when Miles talks about his past and his own situation : he evoques the difficult moments and the contestable choices he made. But he also says that he changed thanks to her and that he is now ready to be here for her no matter the obstacles.

An interesting heroine

Spider-Gwen is mentioned above and it’s interesting to dive a bit more into the character. Actually, it’s a character that is very developed in the movie and that evolves in parallel of Miles. In a way, the song is true from both points of view: as much from Miles to Gwen that the other way around. Obviously, she also loves Miles but fears that some bad thing happens and not being able to protect him because throughout the movie, she tries and fails numerous times.

A coherent message

At the end of the line, Calling is a love song that expresses dedication, putting in evidence the idea of making sacrifices on ourselves and our well-being to protect a loved one. More than being an accurate depiction of the mental state of Miles throughout the movie, it reuses the well-known topic of the super-hero torn between protecting more people or people that counts more. Finally, Calling is mainly a song that puts the focus on love and the engagement toward the other, even in difficult moments.
