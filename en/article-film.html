A film with an unforgettable identity

Posted the : 18/10/2023

Reading time : 4 min

The American animated movie Spiderman: Across the spider-verse, originally planned for April 2022 went out on June 2th in the US and is a great success for an animated movie. Besides, it is distinguished for its unique art style.

More than being a sequel awaited by numerous fans, this movie shines by the uniqueness of its art and animation style. Already present in the first opus: Spiderman : into the spider-verse, it tries to be as close as possible to the sensations and graphics of super-heroes comic books from the 90'. In order to achieve this goal, the movie uses specific techniques to this medium such as frames of half tones and criss-crossed lines for shading and gradients, chromatic aberration to mimic the misaligning of colors that sometimes happen during printing, motion lines also called “smearing” to accentuate motion, and onomatopoeia to accentuate sound effects and emotions.

An unusual framerate

Furthermore, the movie is using a variable display frequency: when most films (animated or not) only uses the usual 24 frames per second method, some plans in Across the spider-verse are deliberately animated in 12 frames per second, giving the animation a choppy look that reminds us the way we follow actions by successively seeing the frames of comic-books pages. But the use of this particular frequency doesn't end there, it's also used in some moments to accentuate emotions like discomfort, lack of confidence or experience.

A melting pot of styles

Actually, the main characters of the movie are coming from different universes and each one have their own, unique art style. The most notable example of this is Spider-punk which aesthetics are inspired by collages and deconstructivism. That way, he holds an irregular and thick outline that represents roughly cut paper, and the different parts that compose him are animated with different framerates (his body is animated every 2 or 3 images, his guitar every 4 images, etc) which translates the anarchy that he represents. Similarly, the film's villain: the Spot is at first represented with a sketchy style and apparent construction lines that shows that he's not complete yet. But at the end of the movie, they disappear to show that he is accomplished as a vilain.

A unique identity

The particular way the visuals are treated, and the attention brought to the graphical distinction between the different universes are exploiting a lot of the possibilities and advantages given by animation and are giving to the film a unique identity. At the end, that is most likely to be the main reason why we will remember Miles Morales.
